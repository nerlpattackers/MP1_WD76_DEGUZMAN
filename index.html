Explore Albay

Albay is a province in the Philippines situated in the Bicol Region occupying the southeastern peninsula of Luzon and the outlying island provinces of Masbate and Catanduanes. Its capital is the City of Legazpi.

The province has a land area of 2,574.91 square kilometers or 994.18 square miles. Its population as determined by the 2020 Census was 1,374,768. This represented 22.60% of the total population of the Bicol Region, 2.21% of the overall population of the Luzon island group, or 1.26% of the entire population of the Philippines. Based on these figures, the population density is computed at 534 inhabitants per square kilometer or 1,383 inhabitants per square mile.

Albay is bordered, clockwise from the North, by Camarines Sur, Lagonoy Gulf, Albay Gulf, Sorsogon, and the Burias Pass.

10 REASONS WHY IT'S MORE FUN IN ALBAY


1. Majestic Mayon Volcano

Albay boasts the stunning Mayon Volcano, an almost perfectly symmetrical cone that dominates the landscape. Its awe-inspiring beauty provides a picturesque backdrop for any activity, making it a photographer's dream.

2. Breathtaking Nature Escapes

From pristine beaches to lush forests and rolling hills, Albay offers an abundance of natural wonders. Whether you're into hiking, swimming, or simply lounging on the beach, there's something for every nature lover.

3. Rich Cultural Heritage

Albay is deeply rooted in history and culture. Explore the Cagsawa Ruins, a historical site that remains as a testament to the destructive power of the Mayon Volcano, or visit the Albay Park and Wildlife to learn about the region's diverse flora and fauna.

4. Delectable Bicolano Cuisine

Bicol is known for its spicy and flavorful cuisine. Savor mouthwatering dishes like Bicol Express, Laing, and Pinangat, which incorporate the region's famous chili peppers and coconut milk.

5. Festivals and Celebrations

Albay is home to various vibrant festivals, such as the Magayon Festival, which celebrates the beauty and resilience of the province. Join in the festivities, witness colorful parades, and immerse yourself in the local culture.

6. Adventure Sports

Thrill-seekers will find their paradise in Albay. Try out exhilarating activities like ATV rides on lava trails, ziplining across lush canyons, or even embark on a challenging trek up Mayon Volcano itself.

7. Warm and Friendly Locals

The locals of Albay are known for their hospitality and warm demeanor. Engaging with them will not only enrich your experience but also create lasting memories of your trip.

8. Eco-Tourism Opportunities

Albay takes pride in its commitment to preserving the environment. Participate in eco-tours that promote sustainability and conservation efforts, allowing you to appreciate the natural beauty responsibly.

9. Unique Souvenirs

Albay is renowned for its handicrafts and products made from abaca fiber, pili nuts, and coconut shells. Take home authentic and locally-made souvenirs to cherish the memories of your time in this captivating province.

10. Vibrant Nightlife and Entertainment

When the sun sets, Albay comes alive with a vibrant nightlife scene. Enjoy live music, dance the night away at local bars and clubs, or join one of the many beach parties and gatherings. The lively atmosphere and friendly locals ensure that your nights in Albay are as fun-filled as your days.

Getting There & Away

Air

There are short flights (roughly 75-90mins) from Manila to Legazpi in Albay, Naga City in Camarines Sur, Virac on Catanduanes and Masbate island. CALABARZON’s Quezon province is also a gateway to Bicol.

Land

By road, it’s about 475km/9hrs 30mins from Manila to Legazpi. Buses, jeepneys and cars are popular means of getting around the region.
