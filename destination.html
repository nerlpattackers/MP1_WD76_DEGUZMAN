Majestic Tourist Spots

Welcome to Albay, a true paradise for lovers of the great outdoors! Situated in the beautiful Philippines, this enchanting province boasts an awe-inspiring landscape that seamlessly combines active volcanoes, majestic mountains, mysterious caves, rugged cliffs, and serene white-sand beaches fringed by vibrant coral reefs.

One of the most striking features of Albay is its active volcanoes, which add a touch of both excitement and wonder to the region. The majestic Mayon Volcano, renowned for its perfect cone shape, dominates the skyline and is an iconic symbol of the province. Despite its potentially dangerous nature, the locals have learned to coexist harmoniously with this geological marvel, and visitors are captivated by its mesmerizing beauty. The occasional mild eruptions serve as a reminder of the powerful forces shaping our planet.

For adventure seekers and avid hikers, the mountains of Albay present an irresistible playground. Numerous trails wind through lush forests and ascending slopes, offering breathtaking panoramas of the surrounding countryside. Explorers can choose from a variety of treks suited to their skill levels, with each trail promising unique experiences and picturesque landscapes that will remain etched in their memories.

Destination


CAGSAWA RUINS

Located in the municipality of Daraga in the province of Albay, Cagsawa Ruins are the remnants of the ‘Cagsaua Church’ that was destroyed by the eruption of Mayon Volcano in 1814. It was built sometime in the 1720s under the supervision of Franciscan Friars. The church was said to be the replacement for the church that was burned by the Dutch in 1636.

CAGRARAY ECO-ENERGY PARK

Cagraray Eco-Energy Park is a project of the Eco-Trail Foundation Society, Inc in partnership with the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR). The 110-hectare eco-park was the first-ever ecological park in the province.

MAYON SKYLINE VIEW DECK

The Mayon Volcano Skyline in Legazpi City, Bicol Region, Philippines offers a breathtaking experience for nature enthusiasts. With its stunning view of the iconic Mayon Volcano, this attraction promises a memorable adventure.

MATAAS BEACH

Nestled along the pristine shores of Bacacay, Albay, Mataas Island Beach is a hidden gem that promises an unforgettable tropical getaway. This serene and unspoiled island paradise beckons travelers with its immaculate stretches of powdery white sand and crystal-clear azure waters that gently caress the shoreline. Accessible by boat, the journey to Mataas Island is an adventure in itself, as visitors are treated to picturesque views of the surrounding sea and lush landscapes.

VERA FALLS

Located in a secluded nook at the southern base of Mt. Malinao is Vera Falls: a paradise of forest and clear cascading water, a little over an hour away from Legazpi. It is a natural treasure to behold and an accessible ecotourism site with a big reward. An exotic 1,200m (1,000 yards) foot trail through lush vegetation awaits trekkers before arriving at the crystal blue lagoon covered under a forest canopy. The sound of the cascading water and native birds heightens the sense of tropical paradise.

8. Eco-Tourism Opportunities

Albay takes pride in its commitment to preserving the environment. Participate in eco-tours that promote sustainability and conservation efforts, allowing you to appreciate the natural beauty responsibly.

9. Unique Souvenirs

Albay is renowned for its handicrafts and products made from abaca fiber, pili nuts, and coconut shells. Take home authentic and locally-made souvenirs to cherish the memories of your time in this captivating province.

10. Vibrant Nightlife and Entertainment

When the sun sets, Albay comes alive with a vibrant nightlife scene. Enjoy live music, dance the night away at local bars and clubs, or join one of the many beach parties and gatherings. The lively atmosphere and friendly locals ensure that your nights in Albay are as fun-filled as your days.

Getting There & Away

Air

There are short flights (roughly 75-90mins) from Manila to Legazpi in Albay, Naga City in Camarines Sur, Virac on Catanduanes and Masbate island. CALABARZON’s Quezon province is also a gateway to Bicol.

Land

By road, it’s about 475km/9hrs 30mins from Manila to Legazpi. Buses, jeepneys and cars are popular means of getting around the region.
